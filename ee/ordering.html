Ordering


Order Placement Guidelines for UST ENGR/EE Labs

Students who need to place orders or project parts (for class, senior design, etc.) should follow these guidelines. Read the entire instructions before placing your order.

  1. Shop wisely

    • Check multiple vendors to be sure you find your item/s at good prices with reasonable shipping charges.
    • Confirm that your items are in stock and ready to ship.
    • Check here for ideas on where to find electronic parts. Preferred vendors that have quick and easy ordering and shipping are Digikey and Amazon. Grainger and McMaster are good too.

  2. Assemble the information I need to place the order

    • Create a shopping cart online

      • Some vendors have shopping carts you can save and forward to me directly.
        • Digikey: Enter your class/team number in the "Customer Reference" Box then send me the "Web ID" and "Access ID" from your order.
        • McMaster: Enter your class/team number in the "Purchase Order" box and send me the requisition by clicking "Forward" in the upper right corner link bar.

      • Print it to a pdf or take a screenshot for inclusion in your email request. YES do this even if you're using one of the direct methods above, it allows me to confirm that I am looking at the right order.

    • If you can't create a shopping cart, send me a spreadsheet.

  3. Email me the information I need to place the order

    • All order-related communications must include the following info (in addition to anything mentioned above):

      • In the Subject line of the email:
        • Team number and Class name (Senior Design, ENGR331, etc)
        • Vendor you are ordering from

      • In the Email:
        • Your name
        • Team number and Class name (Senior Design, ENGR331, etc)
        • Brief written summary of your order (description of items you are ordering). Yes this is necessary even if you are including an attachment or shopping cart access code/link.
        • Complete contact info for the vendor if it's not on my preferred list.

    • Specify how you would like it to be shipped. Be reasonable. Do not ask for rush processing or delivery unless you truly need it and will follow up promptly.

    • Include any other special instructions or information I would need to place the order.

    • I will confirm by reply email when the order has been placed. If you do not get a confirmation from me then assume it did not get ordered. In this case you should follow up with me after a reasonable time to be sure there wasn't a problem.

  4. Follow up properly when the order arrives

    • I will contact you when the order arrives if you have followed my instructions in part 3 above. If you were sloppy in part 3 then I may have trouble finding the details and may not be able to contact you easily. Or I may not have even placed the order.

    • When you receive notice that it has arrived, pick it up promptly or reply to make alternate arrangements.

    • If you need to access the order immediately when it arrives and I have not yet had a chance to check the order in, you must verify that all items on the packing list have arrived in the package, note that on the packing list, and make sure I get the packing list. This should only be done in extreme cases that truly warrant this urgency.

Things to keep in mind:

  • I place several orders for parts for various people every day.
  • I will NOT remember each person's order and details about it between when it is placed and when it arrives.
  • It is important for you to follow the documentation instructions above so that we can get the order in your hands easily and effectively reconcile the billing.
  • I have other duties aside from placing orders, so... do not wait until the last minute to place your order because I may not be available or able to place it instantly.
  • It takes time to receive and process an incoming order, so do not cut it so close that your order is arriving on the day you must have it.
